- Antidepressants: 
  * Tricyclics - increase NE and serotonin
  * MAO inhibitors - most severe side effects, dietary restrictions
  * SSRIs - increase serotonin, milder side effects, faster symptom reduction
  * SNRIs - increase serotonin AND norepinephrine
- CRITICISMS: 90% may be placebo, don't cure, don't teach coping skills, high relapse

B) ECT:
- Sedative + muscle relaxant, brief shock (<1 sec), seizure
- For severe depression with HIGH SUICIDE RISK
- Effects can be IMMEDIATE (faster than meds)
- No one knows why it works
- Criticisms: relapse high, permanent memory loss risk, brain damage risk

C) Psychosurgery:
- Remove/destroy brain tissue
- Used RARELY, last resort

II. PSYCHODYNAMIC THERAPIES:
- Focus: unconscious conflicts
- Goal: INSIGHT (conscious awareness of psychodynamics)
- Techniques: Free Association (uncensored thoughts), Dream Interpretation (unconscious wishes)

III. HUMANISTIC THERAPIES (Carl Rogers):
- Person-centered/Client-centered therapy
- THREE therapist attributes:
  1. Unconditional Positive Regard - care without judgment
  2. Empathy - view world through client's eyes
  3. Genuineness - consistency between feeling and behavior

IV. COGNITIVE THERAPIES:
A) REBT (Ellis) - ABCD Model:
- A = Activating event
- B = Belief system (irrational beliefs cause problems)
- C = Consequences (emotional/behavioral)
- D = Disputing erroneous beliefs

B) Cognitive Therapy (Beck):
- Negative automatic thoughts
- NEGATIVE TRIAD: negative views of SELF, WORLD, FUTURE
- Thoughts (not situation) cause maladaptive reactions

C) CBT:
- Thoughts, feelings, behaviors interconnected
- Uses ABC model
- Challenge unhelpful thoughts, encourage behavioral changes

V. BEHAVIOR THERAPIES:
- Behaviors ARE the problem (not symptoms of underlying issue)
- Behaviors are learned, can be unlearned

A) Exposure Therapy:
- Systematic Desensitization: GRADUAL exposure + relaxation, fear hierarchy
- Flooding: IMMEDIATE intense exposure until anxiety decreases

B) Aversion Therapy:
- Pair attractive stimulus with noxious stimulus
- E.g., alcohol + nausea-inducing drug
- A Clockwork Orange example

C) Token Economy:
- Positive reinforcement (operant conditioning)
- Tokens given for desired behavior, exchanged for reinforcers
