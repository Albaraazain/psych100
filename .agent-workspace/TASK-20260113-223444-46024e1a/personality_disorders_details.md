# Personality Disorders - Comprehensive Details

**Source:** pdf-text-extracted/PSYC100 GENERAL PSYCHOLOGY PSYCHOPATHOLOGY.txt (lines 399-505)

---

## Overview

**10 personality disorders organized into 3 clusters:**
- **Cluster A (Odd/Eccentric):** Paranoid, Schizoid, Schizotypal
- **Cluster B (Dramatic/Emotional/Erratic):** Antisocial, Borderline, Histrionic, Narcissistic
- **Cluster C (Anxious/Fearful):** Avoidant, Dependent, Obsessive-Compulsive

---

## CLUSTER A (Odd or Eccentric)

### 1. Paranoid Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Believes others are exploiting, harming, or deceiving them (without evidence)
- Preoccupied with unjustified doubts about loyalty of friends/colleagues
- Reluctant to confide in others (fears information will be used against them)
- Reads hidden threatening meanings into benign remarks/events
- Bears persistent grudges
- Quick to feel attacked and counterattack

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Persistently suspects coworkers/others are undermining them despite no evidence
- **Cognitive:** Interprets neutral comments as hostile
- **Emotional:** Holds long-standing grudges
- **Social:** Avoids confiding in others due to fears of betrayal

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 35-year-old employee persistently suspects coworkers are undermining him despite no evidence. He interprets neutral comments as hostile, holds longstanding grudges, and avoids confiding in others due to fears of betrayal. Functioning is limited by pervasive mistrust."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Joseph Stalin

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- Unlike schizophrenia: NO hallucinations or delusions (beliefs are suspicious but not delusional)
- Unlike delusional disorder: Suspiciousness is pervasive across many areas, not fixed on one specific delusion
- The mistrust is CONSTANT and PERVASIVE across all relationships

---

### 2. Schizoid Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Neither desires nor enjoys close relationships (including family)
- Almost always chooses solitary activities
- Little or no interest in sexual experiences with another person
- Takes pleasure in few, if any, activities
- Lacks close friends (except possibly first-degree relatives)
- Appears indifferent to praise or criticism
- Emotional coldness, flat affect

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Lives alone, shows little interest in social relationships
- **Cognitive:** Does not desire connection (different from avoidant PD which WANTS connection but fears rejection)
- **Emotional:** Constricted affect, indifferent to praise or criticism
- **Activities:** Reports minimal pleasure from activities

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 40-year-old individual lives alone, shows little interest in social relationships, and reports minimal pleasure from activities. Affect is constricted, and the person appears indifferent to praise or criticism. Occupational functioning is adequate in solitary work settings."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Bill Gates

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Avoidant PD:** Schizoid doesn't WANT relationships; Avoidant WANTS but is too afraid
- **vs. Schizotypal PD:** Schizoid has normal perception; no magical thinking or odd beliefs
- **vs. Autism:** Can function adequately in solitary work; less rigid routines
- The key is LACK OF DESIRE for connection, not fear of it

---

### 3. Schizotypal Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Magical thinking, ideas of reference, odd beliefs
- Unusual perceptual experiences (illusions, sensing a "presence")
- Odd/eccentric appearance or behavior
- Unusual speech (vague, metaphorical, over-elaborate)
- Lack of close friends
- Ideas of reference (not full delusions)

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Limited close relationships, social anxiety
- **Cognitive:** Odd beliefs (e.g., believing they can sense others' thoughts), ideas of reference
- **Perceptual:** Illusions, sensing presence (NOT full hallucinations)
- **Communication:** Vague and metaphorical speech
- **Appearance:** Eccentric behavior and presentation

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 25-year-old individual exhibits social anxiety, eccentric behavior, and odd beliefs (e.g., believing they can sense others' thoughts). Speech is vague and metaphorical, with limited close relationships. Perceptual distortions and magical thinking interfere with social and occupational functioning."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Van Gogh

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Schizophrenia:** NO full delusions or hallucinations; perceptual experiences are "unusual" not psychotic
- **vs. Schizoid PD:** Has odd beliefs and perceptual distortions; schizoid is just detached
- **vs. Paranoid PD:** Magical thinking, not just suspiciousness
- On the "schizophrenia spectrum" but NOT psychotic

## CLUSTER B (Dramatic, Emotional, or Erratic)

### 4. Antisocial Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Pervasive disregard for others' rights
- Deceitful
- Impulsive
- Aggressive
- Reckless
- Zero remorse

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Disregard for others' rights, exploitative
- **Cognitive:** Rationalizes harm, no guilt
- **Emotional:** Lacks remorse for harm caused
- **Behavioral:** Repeated arrests, deceitfulness, impulsive behavior since adolescence
- **Occupational:** Fails to sustain employment or relationships

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 30-year-old individual has a history of repeated arrests, deceitfulness, and impulsive behavior since adolescence. They demonstrate disregard for others' rights, lack remorse for harm caused, and fail to sustain employment or relationships."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Ted Bundy

**Common Presentation:** "Con artists" or criminals who feel no guilt

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **Onset:** Must have evidence of conduct disorder before age 15
- **vs. Narcissistic PD:** Antisocial has NO empathy and actively harms; narcissist exploits but can have some superficial empathy
- **vs. Borderline PD:** Antisocial is calculating and remorseless; borderline acts impulsively from emotional pain
- The defining feature is LACK OF REMORSE and VIOLATION OF OTHERS' RIGHTS

---

### 5. Borderline Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Unstable mood, self-image, and relationships
- Terror of abandonment
- Rapid mood swings
- Chronic emptiness
- Recurrent self-harm or suicidal behavior
- Impulsive (spending, sex, substances)

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Unstable relationships, intense fear of abandonment, alternates between idealizing and devaluing others
- **Cognitive:** Unstable self-image
- **Emotional:** Marked mood reactivity, rapid mood swings, chronic feelings of emptiness
- **Behavioral:** Self-harming behavior during interpersonal stress, impulsive in multiple areas
- **Relational pattern:** Splitting (all-good or all-bad perception of others)

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 26-year-old individual presents with unstable relationships, intense fear of abandonment, and marked mood reactivity. They report chronic feelings of emptiness, engage in self-harming behavior during interpersonal stress, and alternate between idealizing and devaluing others."

**Historical/Celebrity Examples:** Amy Winehouse, Marilyn Monroe

**Metaphor from PDF:** "Emotional burn victims" â€” everything feels life-or-death

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Bipolar Disorder:** BPD mood swings are REACTIVE to interpersonal events and last hours; bipolar episodes last weeks and are not situational
- **vs. Antisocial PD:** BPD self-harms from emotional pain; antisocial harms others without remorse
- **vs. Histrionic PD:** BPD has genuine emotional depth and pain; histrionic emotions are shallow
- The fear of ABANDONMENT drives all behaviors

---

### 6. Histrionic Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Constant attention-seeking
- Overly dramatic/theatrical
- Shallow and rapidly shifting emotions
- Sexually seductive or provocative behavior
- Uncomfortable when not the centre of attention

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Excessive emotionality, attention-seeking behavior, discomfort when not the focus
- **Cognitive:** Perceives relationships as more intimate than they actually are
- **Emotional:** Shallow, rapidly shifting emotions (NOT the deep pain of borderline)
- **Communication:** Speech is impressionistic (lacks detail)
- **Behavioral:** Sexually provocative behavior

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 32-year-old individual displays excessive emotionality, attention-seeking behavior, and discomfort when not the focus of attention. Speech is impressionistic, behavior is sexually provocative, and relationships are perceived as more intimate than they are."

**Historical/Celebrity Examples:** Madonna, Johnny Depp

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Borderline PD:** Histrionic emotions are SHALLOW and dramatic; borderline emotions are DEEP and painful
- **vs. Narcissistic PD:** Histrionic wants ANY attention; narcissist wants ADMIRATION specifically
- **vs. Normal extroversion:** Histrionic is UNCOMFORTABLE when not center of attention; extroverts just enjoy social interaction
- The emotions are like a PERFORMANCE, lacking genuine depth

---

### 7. Narcissistic Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Grandiosity
- Fantasies of unlimited success/power
- Believes they are "special"
- Needs excessive admiration
- Entitlement
- Exploitative
- Lacks empathy
- Envious of others or believes others envy them
- Fragile self-esteem hidden behind arrogance

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Exploits relationships to maintain sense of superiority
- **Cognitive:** Exaggerates achievements, believes they are special
- **Emotional:** Reacts poorly to criticism (narcissistic rage), envious
- **Self-perception:** Grandiosity masking fragile self-esteem
- **Social:** Needs excessive admiration, lacks empathy

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 38-year-old individual exhibits grandiosity, need for admiration, and lack of empathy. They exaggerate achievements, react poorly to criticism, and exploit relationships to maintain a sense of superiority, despite underlying interpersonal difficulties."

**Historical/Celebrity Examples:** Kanye West, Donald Trump, Steve Jobs

**Metaphor from PDF:** "Fragile self-esteem hidden behind arrogance"

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Antisocial PD:** Narcissist wants admiration; antisocial just violates rights without caring
- **vs. Histrionic PD:** Narcissist wants ADMIRATION; histrionic wants general attention
- **vs. Healthy confidence:** Narcissist cannot handle criticism and lacks empathy; confident person can acknowledge flaws
- The FRAGILITY beneath the grandiosity is key

## CLUSTER C (Anxious or Fearful)

### 8. Avoidant Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Extreme social inhibition
- Feelings of inadequacy
- Hypersensitive to criticism or rejection
- Avoids jobs/activities involving contact
- Fears shame
- Sees self as inferior
- Very shy

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Avoids social and occupational interactions, but HAS DESIRE for connection (unlike schizoid)
- **Cognitive:** Perceives self as socially inadequate and inferior
- **Emotional:** Intense fear of criticism and rejection
- **Behavioral:** Engages only in situations where acceptance is assured
- **Occupational:** May avoid jobs requiring social contact

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 29-year-old individual avoids social and occupational interactions due to intense fear of criticism and rejection. Despite a strong desire for connection, they perceive themselves as socially inadequate and engage only in situations where acceptance is assured."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Michael Jackson

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Schizoid PD:** Avoidant WANTS relationships but is too afraid; schizoid has no desire
- **vs. Social Anxiety Disorder:** Avoidant is pervasive across ALL areas of life and self-identity; social anxiety is specific to performance situations
- **vs. Dependent PD:** Avoidant avoids relationships from fear of rejection; dependent clings to relationships from fear of being alone
- The key is WANTING connection but being PARALYZED by fear of rejection

---

### 9. Dependent Personality Disorder

**Core Features:**
- Excessive need to be taken care of
- Submissive and clinging behavior
- Terror of separation
- Lets others take responsibility
- Fears being alone
- Urgently seeks new relationship when one ends
- Difficulty disagreeing

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Submissive and clinging behavior, remains in unhealthy relationships to avoid abandonment
- **Cognitive:** Believes they cannot function without others
- **Emotional:** Fear of being alone, terror of separation
- **Decision-making:** Difficulty making decisions without reassurance, lets others take responsibility
- **Relational:** Urgently seeks new relationship when one ends

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 34-year-old individual demonstrates excessive need to be cared for, leading to submissive and clinging behavior. They have difficulty making decisions without reassurance, fear being alone, and remain in unhealthy relationships to avoid abandonment."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Scott Disick

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. Borderline PD:** Dependent is consistently submissive and clinging; borderline alternates between clinging and pushing away
- **vs. Avoidant PD:** Dependent actively seeks relationships (submissive); avoidant avoids relationships (fearful)
- **vs. Normal interdependence:** Dependent CANNOT make decisions alone; normal people can but prefer input
- The defining feature is inability to function WITHOUT others taking care of them

---

### 10. Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD)

**Core Features:**
- Rigid perfectionism, orderliness, and control at the expense of flexibility and efficiency
- Preoccupied with rules/lists
- Stubborn perfectionism
- Workaholic
- Miserly (stingy with money)
- Hoarding
- Difficulty delegating unless done exactly their way

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- **Interpersonal:** Experiences interpersonal conflict due to rigidity and high standards, struggles to delegate
- **Cognitive:** Preoccupied with orderliness, perfectionism, and control
- **Emotional:** Rigid, inflexible
- **Behavioral:** Devotes excessive time to work, hoarding, miserly
- **Occupational:** Perfectionism interferes with task completion

**Clinical Example from PDF:**
"A 42-year-old individual is preoccupied with orderliness, perfectionism, and control, at the expense of flexibility and efficiency. They devote excessive time to work, struggle to delegate tasks, and experience interpersonal conflict due to rigidity and high standards."

**Historical/Celebrity Example:** Elon Musk

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- **vs. OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder):**
  - **OCPD:** EGO-SYNTONIC (person thinks their perfectionism is GOOD and necessary)
  - **OCD:** EGO-DYSTONIC (person recognizes thoughts/compulsions are EXCESSIVE and wants them to stop)
  - **OCPD:** Rigid personality pattern
  - **OCD:** Specific anxiety disorder with intrusive thoughts and compulsions
- **vs. Healthy conscientiousness:** OCPD perfectionism INTERFERES with efficiency; healthy person knows when "good enough" is acceptable
- **vs. Narcissistic PD:** OCPD focuses on rules/order; narcissist focuses on admiration
- The rigidity and control come at the EXPENSE of flexibility and efficiency

## Cluster Differences Summary

### CLUSTER A (Odd/Eccentric)
**Common thread:** Social isolation and odd thinking
- **Paranoid:** Mistrustful and suspicious
- **Schizoid:** Detached and uninterested
- **Schizotypal:** Eccentric with perceptual oddities

### CLUSTER B (Dramatic/Emotional/Erratic)
**Common thread:** Emotional dysregulation and impulsivity
- **Antisocial:** Violates others' rights without remorse
- **Borderline:** Intense emotions and fear of abandonment
- **Histrionic:** Shallow emotions and attention-seeking
- **Narcissistic:** Grandiosity and need for admiration

### CLUSTER C (Anxious/Fearful)
**Common thread:** Anxiety and fear driving behavior
- **Avoidant:** Wants connection but fears rejection
- **Dependent:** Needs others to function
- **OCPD:** Needs control and perfection

## Important Clinical Notes

1. **Personality disorders are PERVASIVE patterns** - they affect multiple areas of life (relationships, work, self-perception), not just isolated behaviors

2. **Ego-syntonic vs. Ego-dystonic:**
   - Most personality disorders are EGO-SYNTONIC (person doesn't see the pattern as problematic)
   - This makes treatment challenging

3. **Onset:** Usually evident by adolescence or early adulthood and persists throughout life

4. **Functional impairment:** The patterns must cause significant distress or impairment

5. **Cultural context matters:** What's considered "odd" or "dramatic" varies by culture

6. **Comorbidity is common:** Many people meet criteria for multiple personality disorders, especially within the same cluster

## Question-Writing Focus Areas

### Concrete behaviors to test understanding:
1. **Cluster differentiation:** Given a behavior, identify which cluster
2. **Within-cluster distinctions:** What makes paranoid different from schizoid?
3. **Ego-syntonic vs ego-dystonic:** OCPD vs OCD distinction
4. **Fear vs. desire patterns:** Avoidant wants connection but fears rejection; schizoid has no desire
5. **Celebrity/historical examples:** Stalin (paranoid), Ted Bundy (antisocial), etc.
6. **Clinical scenarios:** Apply diagnostic criteria to case vignettes
7. **Interpersonal patterns:** How each disorder affects relationships differently
8. **Distinguishing from other disorders:** NPD vs antisocial, BPD vs bipolar, etc.

### Tricky distinctions to test:
- Schizoid (no desire) vs. Avoidant (desires but fears)
- Borderline (deep emotions, abandonment) vs. Histrionic (shallow emotions, attention)
- Narcissistic (admiration) vs. Histrionic (general attention)
- OCPD (personality pattern, ego-syntonic) vs. OCD (anxiety disorder, ego-dystonic)
- Antisocial (no remorse) vs. Narcissistic (fragile ego)
- Borderline mood swings (hours, reactive) vs. Bipolar (weeks, not situational)
- Schizotypal (odd beliefs, ideas of reference) vs. Schizophrenia (full delusions/hallucinations)

### Mechanism-focused explanations needed:
- WHY does borderline person split (all-good/all-bad)?
- HOW does narcissist's fragile self-esteem drive grandiosity?
- WHAT maintains avoidant's pattern (fear reinforces avoidance)?
- WHY does dependent person urgently seek new relationships?
- HOW does antisocial person rationalize harmful behavior?
